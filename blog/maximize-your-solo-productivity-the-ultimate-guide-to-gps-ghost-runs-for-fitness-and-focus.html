GPS Ghost Runs: What’s All the Buzz About?

Think about your typical workout. You might jog around the block, hit the gym, or return to the comforts of your home workout routine. But have you ever considered “ghost running”? This feature is all about competing against your past self. Here’s how it works:

When you go for a run, your app tracks your pace, distance, and stats. The next time you lace up your sneakers, your app will set a 'ghost' of your past self to race against. It’s like having a personal rival motivating you to beat your own best time! The bonus? It’s not just about rushing through a workout—this can really keep you focused and engaged.

The Psychology Behind Ghost Runs

But wait, why does racing against your own past efforts make you more productive and fit? Here’s the juicy part:

  1. Increased Motivation: Knowing you are competing against your past self can add a competitive edge to your workout. You’re not just running for the sake of running—you’re running to beat YOU!

  2. Building Consistency: Ghost runs promote consistency in your training. When you have a visible target—like your previous best—it pushes you to put in the work regularly.

  3. Enhanced Mindfulness: This element brings a new level of focus to your workouts. You’re thinking about your performance and how to improve it by paying attention to pace, form, and energy levels.
