What are GPS Ghost Runs?

Alright, let’s break it down. GPS Ghost Runs are essentially a way for you to race against yourself. Imagine lacing up your sneakers, hitting the trail, and challenging your past self to a rematch! With GPS tracking built into the Solo Productivity app, you can track your past runs. When you do a Ghost Run, you’re racing against that recorded time. Cool, right?

This method not only amps up your fitness game but also enhances focus. You’re not just running; you’re setting goals, pushing limits, and getting that sweet dopamine hit when you beat your own time. It’s like being your own coach, but without the sweaty yelling.

Why You Should Incorporate Ghost Runs into Your Routine

As a casual indie developer, let me tell you, it’s not just about getting those miles in. There are massive benefits to incorporating Ghost Runs into your productivity arsenal:

1. Improved Accountability

When you know you’re racing against your previous self, there’s a sense of accountability that comes into play. You’re effectively committing to push your boundaries, which translates to a more focused approach—both on the run and in other areas of your life.

2. Progress Tracking

Want to see how far you've come? Ghost Runs give you a tangible measure of progress. You can check your previous times, see where you can improve, and celebrate the successes. It’s all about that growth mindset, my friends!

3. Enhanced Motivation

Nothing gets the blood pumping like a little competition—especially when it’s with yourself! Every time you lace up for a Ghost Run, you’re getting that little boost of motivation. Plus, it’s a solid way to break monotony and keep things exciting.

4. Boosted Mental Focus

Running isn't just physical; it’s incredibly mental too. Setting a performance goal for yourself, like besting your last time, can sharpen your focus—not just while you’re on the track, but throughout your day. It becomes a healthy cycle of establishing goals and achieving them.
